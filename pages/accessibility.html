Accessibility Statement

Our commitment to providing an inclusive and accessible digital experience for all users.

The University of Toronto Temerty Faculty of Medicine Alumni Association is committed to ensuring that our website and digital services are accessible to all people, including those with disabilities. We strive to meet or exceed the requirements of the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA) and the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA.

Our Commitment

As part of the University of Toronto community, we are dedicated to creating an inclusive environment that enables all individuals to access and benefit from our alumni services. We recognize that accessibility is an ongoing effort, and we are continually working to improve the user experience for everyone who visits our website.

Accessibility Features

Our website includes the following accessibility features:

  • Semantic HTML: We use proper heading structures, landmarks, and semantic elements to ensure screen readers can navigate our content effectively.
  • Keyboard Navigation: All interactive elements on our site are accessible via keyboard, allowing users to navigate without a mouse.
  • Alternative Text: Images include descriptive alternative text to convey information to users who rely on screen readers.
  • Colour Contrast: We maintain sufficient colour contrast ratios between text and background elements to ensure readability for users with low vision or colour vision deficiencies.
  • Responsive Design: Our website adapts to various screen sizes and devices, including mobile phones, tablets, and desktop computers.
  • Clear Typography: We use readable fonts and appropriate text sizes to improve legibility across all devices.
  • Focus Indicators: Visible focus indicators are provided for interactive elements to assist keyboard users in navigating the site.

AODA Compliance

The Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA) sets out requirements for organizations in Ontario to identify, remove, and prevent barriers to accessibility. Our website is designed to comply with the Integrated Accessibility Standards Regulation (IASR) under the AODA, including the requirements for accessible websites and web content.

We regularly review our website against WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards and address any identified issues in a timely manner.

Requesting Accommodations

We are committed to providing accommodations to ensure that all alumni can fully participate in our programs and services. If you require an accommodation for an event, service, or to access content on our website, please contact us as early as possible so we can make appropriate arrangements.

Accommodations may include but are not limited to:

  • Providing documents in alternative formats (large print, Braille, electronic text)
  • Arranging sign language interpretation for events
  • Ensuring physical accessibility at in-person events
  • Providing captioning or transcripts for audio and video content
  • Offering alternative methods for completing online forms or registrations

Feedback

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of our website and services. If you encounter any accessibility barriers, have suggestions for improvement, or wish to request content in an alternative format, please let us know. Your input helps us continue to improve the accessibility of our digital presence.

When providing feedback, please include:

  • The web page address (URL) where you encountered the issue
  • A description of the accessibility barrier you experienced
  • The assistive technology you were using, if applicable
  • Your preferred method of contact so we can follow up with you

We will acknowledge receipt of your feedback within two business days and work to resolve the issue as quickly as possible.
